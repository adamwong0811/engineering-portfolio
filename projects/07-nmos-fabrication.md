## Project Summary

This project executed and documented a CMOS-compatible NMOS transistor fabrication flow. The work connected semiconductor theory to real process steps: oxidation, photolithography, diffusion, metallization, oxide measurement, and electrical characterization.

## Process Flow

- Silicon wafer preparation.
- Wet/dry thermal oxidation to form oxide layers.
- UV photolithography using multiple mask steps.
- Phosphorus diffusion for n-type source/drain formation.
- Aluminum metallization.
- Etching and post-process inspection.
- Profilometer oxide thickness measurement.
- Probe-station and parametric-analyzer I-V characterization.

## Key Results

| Measurement | Result |
| --- | --- |
| Average oxide thickness before first photolithography | 4844.6 A |
| Average oxide thickness after dry oxidation | 5178.4 A |
| Electrical test | Functional NMOS I-V behavior observed |
| Drain voltage sweep | 0 V to 3 V in recorded measurement table |
| Device behavior | Saturation-region behavior and process-induced variation observed |

## What I Learned

- How individual process steps influence final transistor characteristics.
- Why oxide thickness, doping, mask alignment, and metallization quality are critical to device behavior.
- How to use I-V measurements to verify that a fabricated device behaves like an NMOS transistor.
- How process variation appears in measured electrical data.
