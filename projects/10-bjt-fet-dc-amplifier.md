# BJT vs FET DC Amplifier Comparative Analysis

**CV priority:** 10  
**Date:** October 2025  
**Type:** Analog Electronics Laboratory  
**Tools:** Multisim, breadboard, DMM  
**Source report:** `AE - Lab 2 (2).pdf`

## Project Summary

This experiment compared BJT and FET amplifier biasing and DC performance using theoretical calculation, simulation, and bench measurement. The work focused on understanding how device physics affects circuit behavior.

## Circuits Studied

- 2N3904 BJT common-emitter amplifier.
- BS170P FET common-source amplifier.
- Bias-network calculation and DC operating-point validation.
- Simulation and hardware measurement comparison.

## Key Results

| Metric | Result |
| --- | --- |
| BJT device | 2N3904 |
| FET device | BS170P |
| BJT common-emitter voltage gain | About -2.97 |
| FET common-source voltage gain | About -2.71 |
| Main comparison | BJT offers higher gain and predictable transconductance; FET offers high input impedance |

## What I Learned

- How BJT and FET amplifier biasing differ in practice.
- Why transconductance drives voltage-gain differences.
- How component tolerance, temperature, and device parameter spread affect measured results.
- How to validate DC operating points before judging gain behavior.
